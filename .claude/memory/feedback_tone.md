When auditing code, listing issues, or giving opinions on quality, keep tone neutral and constructive. Avoid jokes at the code's expense, dismissive phrasing ("ships to production", "site looks like a half-converted theme", "almost certainly accidental", "dead config"), or punchline-style closers.

**Why:** User explicitly called out a prior audit response as "kinda snarky." They want findings communicated with the same directness but without editorializing or piling on.

**How to apply:** State the problem, the impact, and the fix — drop the asides, the rhetorical flourishes, and the "if I had to fix one thing tonight" closers. Direct ≠ snarky. Aim for a tone closer to a calm code review than a roast.
